## Technical Background
The development of AI-proof fonts is rooted in the understanding of how AI models, particularly those based on neural networks, process and recognize text. Most modern AI models, including popular language models like GPT-3.5 and Claude, rely on complex neural network architectures to analyze and generate text. These models are trained on vast amounts of data, which enables them to recognize patterns and relationships within text. However, this reliance on pattern recognition also creates a vulnerability that can be exploited to create AI-proof fonts.

The AI-proof font, which we'll refer to as "SanSerif," utilizes a combination of techniques to evade detection by AI models. One key technique employed by SanSerif is the use of subtle distortions and variations in character shapes, which are imperceptible to the human eye but significant enough to confuse AI models. For example, the font's designers have introduced minor changes to the curvature of letterforms, the width of strokes, and the positioning of diacritical marks. These changes are designed to disrupt the patterns that AI models use to recognize text, making it difficult for them to accurately read and understand the text.
