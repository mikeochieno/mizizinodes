## Introduction to Waze's AI-Powered Features
Waze, the popular navigation app acquired by Google in 2013, has long been known for its user-generated traffic data and real-time routing updates. The recent introduction of AI-powered features takes Waze's capabilities to the next level, enabling the app to learn from user behavior, adapt to changing traffic patterns, and provide more accurate predictions. At the heart of this development is a machine learning model that analyzes vast amounts of data, including traffic speed, road conditions, and user feedback.

## Comparison with Previous Approaches
Waze's AI-powered features can be compared to other navigation systems, such as Google Maps and Apple Maps, which also employ machine learning algorithms to optimize routing. However, Waze's approach differs in its emphasis on user-generated data and real-time updates. For instance, Waze's model is trained on a dataset of over 100 million user reports per day, allowing it to adapt quickly to changing traffic conditions. In contrast, Google Maps relies on a more comprehensive dataset that includes satellite imagery and street view data, but may not be as responsive to real-time changes.
