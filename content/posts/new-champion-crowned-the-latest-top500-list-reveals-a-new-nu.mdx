## Introduction to the TOP500 List
The TOP500 list is a bi-annual ranking of the world's most powerful supercomputers, released at the International Supercomputing Conference (ISC). The list is a benchmark for the high-performance computing (HPC) community, showcasing the latest advancements in supercomputing technology. The rankings are based on the LINPACK benchmark, which measures a system's ability to solve a large system of linear equations.

## The New Number 1 Supercomputer
At the ISC'26 conference, the latest TOP500 list was revealed, and a new supercomputer has taken the top spot. The new number 1 supercomputer, named "Frontier," is located at the Oak Ridge National Laboratory in the United States. Frontier is a Cray Shasta system, powered by AMD EPYC 7003 processors and NVIDIA A100 GPUs. With a record-breaking performance of 1.102 exaflops, Frontier has surpassed the previous top-ranked supercomputer, Summit, also located at Oak Ridge.

## Performance and Capabilities
Frontier's impressive performance is a result of its advanced architecture and cutting-edge technology.
