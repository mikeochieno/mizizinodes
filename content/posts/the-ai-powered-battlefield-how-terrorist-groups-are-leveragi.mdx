## Context: The History of Asymmetric Warfare
The use of AI by terrorist groups is not a new phenomenon, but rather the latest iteration of asymmetric warfare. Asymmetric warfare refers to the use of unconventional tactics and strategies by non-state actors to counter the military superiority of conventional forces. Throughout history, terrorist groups have leveraged various technologies to achieve their goals, from the use of improvised explosive devices (IEDs) in Iraq and Afghanistan to the deployment of drones in Syria and Ukraine. The adoption of AI-powered tools is simply the next step in this evolution, as terrorist groups seek to exploit the advantages of machine learning and automation to enhance their military capabilities.
