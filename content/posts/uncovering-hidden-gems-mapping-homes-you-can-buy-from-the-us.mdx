## How the Government Sells Homes
The US government sells homes through various agencies, including the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), and the Department of Agriculture (USDA). These agencies acquire properties through foreclosure, tax defaults, or other means, and then sell them to the public at discounted prices. The goal is to recoup some of the losses and provide affordable housing options to low- and moderate-income families.
