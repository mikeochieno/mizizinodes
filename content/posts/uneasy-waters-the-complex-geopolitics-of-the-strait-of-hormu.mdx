## Introduction
The Strait of Hormuz, a narrow waterway situated between Iran and Oman, is one of the most strategic and contested regions in the world. Approximately 20% of the world's oil passes through this strait, making it a critical chokepoint for global energy supplies. In recent years, the area has been plagued by tensions between Iran and the United States, as well as other regional players. The latest developments, including the seizure of ships and the presence of shark fishermen, have added a new layer of complexity to the situation.

## A History of Tensions
The Strait of Hormuz has long been a source of contention between Iran and its neighbors. In the 1980s, during the Iran-Iraq War, the strait was a key battleground, with both sides attacking oil tankers and other ships. More recently, the region has been the site of numerous incidents, including the seizure of a British-flagged oil tanker by Iranian forces in 2019. The incident sparked a significant escalation of tensions between Iran and the UK, and highlighted the vulnerability of shipping in the region.
