While TRMNL represents a significant advancement in AI agents, it's essential to acknowledge its limitations and potential drawbacks. One of the primary concerns is the complexity of the hybrid architecture, which can make it challenging to interpret and understand the decision-making process of the model. Additionally, the large size of the model and the extensive training data required can make it difficult to deploy and maintain in resource-constrained environments.

Furthermore, the use of diffusion models in TRMNL raises questions about the potential for mode collapse, a phenomenon where the model produces limited variations of the same output. This can be mitigated through careful tuning of the model's hyperparameters and the use of techniques such as regularization and early stopping.
